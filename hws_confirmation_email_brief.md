## Email Goals

1. **Confirm the spot** — make them feel like they made the right call. Warm, not bureaucratic.
2. **Reduce friction** — give them everything they need in one email so there's no hunting around before April 19.
3. **Build anticipation** — this isn't just a calendar invite. Something real is starting.
4. **Establish the container** — introduce the Telegram group as the community space, not just a chat.
5. **Prime them to show up** — a gentle instruction to do one thing before Sunday.

## Copy Directives

### Tone
- Warm, direct, personal — Sophia's voice, not a brand blast
- Write like you're talking to one person, not a list
- No corporate language ("you have successfully registered") — human all the way
- Short sentences. Room to breathe.

### Structure
1. **Opening line** — land the confirmation immediately, warmly. They're in.
2. **What's coming** — brief program arc: onboarding + 3 days. Don't over-explain.
3. **The links** — Meet link, Telegram invite, guidebook. Clearly labeled. No burying.
4. **Session schedule** — all four dates, times in US timezones + Argentina
5. **One thing to do before Sunday** — a single gentle prompt (e.g. notice your screen use today — no judgment, just observe)
6. **Closing** — personal, brief. Signed as Sophia, not "the HWS team"

### What to avoid
- Overwhelming them with instructions
- Making it feel like a terms & conditions email
- Over-explaining the methodology — save that for the sessions
- Any shame language around screen use
- Exclamation points everywhere — let the warmth come from the words, not the punctuation

### Subject line options (pick one)
- you're in. see you april 19.
- your spot is confirmed — here's everything you need
- it starts sunday. here's how to prepare.
- we're glad you're here.

### Preview text (shows under subject line in inbox)
- your meet link, telegram invite, and guidebook are all inside.

## One Thing to Do Before Building This Email

Read the intake responses from the first registrants before writing the final copy. Even 3–5 responses will tell you the exact language your audience uses about their screen habits — and you can mirror it back in the email. That's what makes it feel like it was written for them specifically.
